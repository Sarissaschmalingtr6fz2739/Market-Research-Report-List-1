<p><p>Microturbine generators are classified into two main types based on their design: unrecuperated microturbines and recuperated microturbines. Unrecuperated microturbines do not utilize waste heat recovery systems, resulting in lower overall efficiency but lower initial costs. On the other hand, recuperated microturbines incorporate waste heat recovery technology to increase overall efficiency and reduce energy costs. Both types of microturbines are used in various applications including combined heat and power (CHP) systems, standby power generation, and distributed energy generation.</p></p>
