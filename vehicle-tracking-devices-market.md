<p><p>Vehicle tracking devices are categorized into two main types based on the technology used for communication and tracking. Cellular network-based trackers use mobile networks to transmit location data in real-time, offering coverage in urban areas where cell towers are prevalent. On the other hand, satellite-based trackers rely on GPS technology to transmit location information through satellite signals, providing coverage in remote areas where cellular networks may not reach. Both types of trackers serve the same purpose of monitoring vehicle movements and enhancing safety and security.</p></p>
