<p><ul><li>Sound Signal Detection</li><li>Blood Flow Signal Detection</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-invasive Intracranial Pressure (ICP) monitoring devices can be classified into two main types: Sound Signal Detection and Blood Flow Signal Detection. Sound Signal Detection devices use technologies such as tympanic membrane displacement to measure ICP, without the need for invasive procedures. On the other hand, Blood Flow Signal Detection devices utilize technologies like transcranial Doppler ultrasound to monitor changes in blood flow, which can indicate variations in ICP. Both types of devices are non-invasive methods for monitoring ICP and offer benefits in terms of reduced risk and patient comfort.</p></p>
