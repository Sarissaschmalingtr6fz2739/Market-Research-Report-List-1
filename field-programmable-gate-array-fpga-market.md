<p><p>A Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) is an integrated circuit that can be programmed or reprogrammed after manufacturing. It consists of an array of programmable logic blocks that can be configured to perform specific tasks and an interconnect structure that allows the logic blocks to communicate with each other. FPGA technology offers flexibility, high performance, low power consumption, and faster time-to-market compared to traditional Application Specific Integrated Circuits (ASICs) or processors.</p><p>The global FPGA market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. Factors driving this growth include the increasing demand for high-performance computing, the rising adoption of advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence and machine learning, and the need for quicker and more efficient data processing. There is also an increasing interest in integrating FPGA technology with Artificial Intelligence (AI) accelerators to improve performance in AI-based applications.
