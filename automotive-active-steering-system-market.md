<p><p>Automotive active steering systems can be categorized into two main types: Active Front Axle Steering and Active Rear Axle Steering. Active Front Axle Steering involves steering interventions at the front wheels to improve handling and stability, while Active Rear Axle Steering involves steering interventions at the rear wheels to enhance responsiveness and agility. Both systems work in conjunction with the traditional steering system in a vehicle to provide advanced control and maneuverability, leading to improved driving dynamics and overall driving experience.</p></p>
