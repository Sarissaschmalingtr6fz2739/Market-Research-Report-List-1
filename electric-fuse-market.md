<p><p>Electric fuse market can be segmented into high voltage fuses and low voltage fuses. High voltage fuses are designed to protect electrical systems with voltages exceeding 1,000 volts, such as industrial machinery and power distribution systems. On the other hand, low voltage fuses are used in circuits with voltages below 1,000 volts, commonly found in residential and commercial buildings. Both types of fuses play a crucial role in preventing electrical fires and equipment damage by interrupting the flow of excessive current.</p></p>
