<p><p>Low-k dielectric materials are categorized based on their dielectric constant. Materials with a dielectric constant of less than 3 are considered ultra-low-k, offering the lowest levels of signal distortion and crosstalk. Meanwhile, materials with a dielectric constant between 3 and 4 are classified as low-k, providing a balance between performance and cost. Both types of low-k materials are widely used in the semiconductor industry to reduce power consumption, improve signal integrity, and enhance overall device performance.</p></p>
