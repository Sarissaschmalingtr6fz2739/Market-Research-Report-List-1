<p><p>Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) materials can be classified into two main market types based on their structure: two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D). 2D COFs consist of single-layered structures, offering properties like flexibility and a high surface area, making them ideal for applications such as gas storage and separation. On the other hand, 3D COFs have a more complex, interconnected structure, providing enhanced stability and conductivity, making them suitable for applications like catalysis and energy storage. Both types of COFs offer unique advantages and are driving advancements in various industries.</p></p>
